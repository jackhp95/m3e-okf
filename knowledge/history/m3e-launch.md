Google announced **Material 3 Expressive (M3E)** in **May 2025**, with fuller detail at
Google I/O later that month. The framing was that M3E makes interfaces more personal,
emotive, and intuitive while staying grounded in usability.

## What was announced

M3E is positioned as an **evolution of Material 3**, not a new generation: it keeps M3's
tokens, color roles, and component set and layers on three foundational shifts —
physics-based [motion](/expressive/motion-physics), an expanded
[shape](/expressive/shape-morphing) system with morphing, and an
[emphasized typography](/expressive/emphasized-type) scale — plus a refreshed and expanded
component suite (button groups, split buttons, toolbars, loading indicators, an updated
FAB, and more). See [what changed](/expressive/what-changed).

## Backed by research

Unusually for a visual refresh, M3E launched alongside a published research program:
Google reported it was tuned through 46 studies with more than 18,000 participants, and
cited eye-tracking evidence that people found key elements up to four times faster in
expressive designs. That evidence — and its "well applied" caveat — is covered in
[research rationale](/expressive/research-rationale).

## First platforms

The launch paired M3E with **Android 16** and **Wear OS 6**, with early access on Pixel
devices and via new Jetpack Compose libraries for phones and watches. The broader story of
how it reached other platforms — and where the **web** lagged — is in
[platform rollout](/history/platform-rollout) and
[web implementation gap](/history/web-implementation-gap).
