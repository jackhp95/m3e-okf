Material Design has passed through three named generations. Google later gave them
retroactive version numbers, so the same system is referred to as M1, M2, and M3.

## Material Design 1 (2014)

Google introduced Material Design at Google I/O in **2014**. It framed a visual language
around a metaphor of "material" — surfaces with position in space, realistic light and
shadow (elevation), and motion that respected physical plausibility. The emphasis was a
coherent, opinionated system: a fixed palette approach, a defined type scale, and
consistent components across Android, web, and beyond.

## Material Theming — M2 (2018)

At I/O **2018**, Google introduced **Material Theming**, later designated M2. The shift
was toward brand flexibility: teams could customize color, typography, and shape to look
like *their* product while staying within the system's usability principles. Tools like
a theme builder made that customization systematic rather than ad hoc.

## Material You / Material 3 (2021)

At I/O **2021**, Google announced **Material You**, the system officially named
**Material 3 (M3)**. Its signature idea was **dynamic color**: a color scheme generated
from the user's wallpaper (via an extraction engine known as Monet), making the UI feel
personal to each user. M3 also formalized the versioning — naming the new system M3 and
retroactively labeling the 2018 era M2 and the 2014 original M1 — and rebuilt the token
and color-role systems this bundle's [foundations](/foundations) and [styles](/styles)
describe.

## Toward Expressive

M3 continued to evolve. In 2025 Google introduced **Material 3 Expressive**, an evolution
of M3 rather than a fourth generation — same tokens and roles, plus a more emotive,
attention-guiding surface. That story is told in [M3E launch](/history/m3e-launch) and
[what changed](/expressive/what-changed).
