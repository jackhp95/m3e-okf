The text in a UI is part of the design. Content design covers text alternatives
for images, the microcopy on components (labels, errors, hints), and the writing
style that ties a product together.

## Alt text

A meaningful image needs a text alternative that conveys its purpose rather than
its appearance; a purely decorative image takes an empty one so assistive
technology skips it.

## Microcopy on components

A label says what an action does and stays short and action-oriented. Error text
states what went wrong and how to fix it, not merely that something is invalid.
Hints sit beside a field to guide entry.

## Consistent terminology

One action carries one name across the whole product. Sentence case, second
person, active voice, and avoiding jargon the user would not use all keep the
writing clear and consistent.
